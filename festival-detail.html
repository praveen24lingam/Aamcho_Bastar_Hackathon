Bastar Dussehra

The Longest Festival of the World - Celebrating 75 Days of Culture

Back to Festivals

About the Festival

Bastar Dussehra is a unique cultural trait of Chhattisgarh. Unlike the rest of India, where Dussehra is about the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya, Bastar Dussehra is entirely dedicated to the local goddess, Danteshwari Devi.

It is renowned as the longest festival in the world, spanning over 75 days. It is a brilliant amalgamation of tribal culture, royal traditions, and deep-rooted spirituality, bringing together various tribes of the Bastar region.

Key Highlights

Traditional Tribal Music
Tribal Dances & Costumes
Chariot Pulling (Rath Yatra)
Sacred Rituals

Festival Schedule

  • Pata Jatra (August/September)

    Worship of wood brought from the forest to build the chariot.

  • Deri Gadhai

    Fixing of the pillars for the construction of the chariot.

  • Kachan Gaadi

    Seeking permission from Goddess Kachan Devi to start the festival.

  • Nisha Jatra & Rath Parikrama

    The grand pulling of the massive wooden chariot by tribal members.
